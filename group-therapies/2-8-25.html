üéØ Visual Tracking Activity

Setup: A large width line tape (brown or colored) is drawn on the floor, starting with a straight path that continues into a zigzag pattern creating a challenging course.

Process: Each child is given a stick with a disc cone attached. With their parent's gentle assistance and guidance, the child must carefully track and follow the drawn line from start to finish, maintaining focus and coordination throughout the journey.

Equipment: Large width line tape, Medicine ball, Green stick with disc cone
Therapeutic Goals & Benefits:
Hand-Eye Coordination: Develops precise visual-motor integration as children learn to coordinate what they see with their hand movements
Body Balance: Strengthens core stability and proprioceptive awareness while navigating the path
Visual Processing: Enhances visual tracking skills and spatial awareness
Focus & Attention: Improves sustained attention and concentration abilities

üë£ Point-to-Point Foot Coordination

Setup: Plastic glasses are arranged in a straight line with colorful sponge balls placed alternately beside each glass, creating an engaging obstacle course.

Process: Children use their feet to carefully pick up each sponge ball and drop it into the corresponding glass. Parents provide supportive assistance throughout, encouraging and guiding their child's movements while celebrating each successful attempt.

Equipment: Colorful sponge balls, Plastic glasses
Therapeutic Goals & Benefits:
Foot Grasping Skills: Develops fine motor control in the feet and toes, strengthening intrinsic foot muscles
Foot-Eye Coordination: Enhances the connection between visual input and lower extremity motor output
Body Balance & Stability: Improves single-leg stance and dynamic balance while performing the task
Self-Confidence: Builds self-esteem through successful task completion and positive reinforcement
Bilateral Coordination: Develops coordination between both sides of the body
Problem-Solving: Encourages strategic thinking and motor planning skills
